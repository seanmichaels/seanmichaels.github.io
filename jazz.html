Jazz

The album Jazz was one of the most popular albums made by Queen. It was released on November 10, 1978 by EMI Records in the United Kingdom and also by Elektra Records in the United States. It was produced by Roy Thomas Baker, and the album artwork was suggested by Roger Taylor, who previously saw a similar design painted on the Berlin Wall. The album's varying musical styles were both praised and criticised. The songs that were on the album is as follows: Mustapha, Fat Bottomed Girls, Jealous, Bicycle Race, If You Can't Beat Them, Let me entertain you, Dead on Time, In only Seven Days, Dreamer's Ball, Fun It, Leaving Home Ain't Easy, Don't stop Me Now, and More of that Jazz. It was second on the UK Albums Chart, and it also was sixth on the US Billboard 200. The album Jazz, has sold over 5 million copies worldwide.
